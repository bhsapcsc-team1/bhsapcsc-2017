Tips for school success:

  • Make good friends
    • Good friends will help you out when you feel down in the dumps
    • Good friends will help you study when you have trouble in a class
  • Turn in Classwork on time
    • Some teachers will not accept late work
    • Most teachers will severely reduce credit on late work
  • Do your readings for class
    • Many student will read a summary online for required reading books
    • Don’t do this
    • Most teachers will make sure test have more specific information than these summaries give the reader
    • Use these resources as a confirmation and support of your reading
  • Don’t get too stressed!
    • While academic life is important, your mental wellbeing should come first.
    • If you’re feeling stressed, take a study break!
    • Remember to set aside time for your social life. School is important, but so are developing your social skills.
  • Feeling overburdened? Ask for Help!
    • Your teachers are there to help you, ask to make an appointment to have them help you with something!
    • Friends can also be good teachers, ask them for help if they’re good in the area you are having trouble with.
    • Go to BYS for non academic counselling help and other resources there to help you provided by the community.
  • Stay safe and happy
    • Make sure you know the school rules
    • There are various rules related to dress code that you must follow if you do not want to be asked to change or go home.
  • Finish Homework in any time you get
    • Sports and clubs can be stressful if you are behind of school work
    • If your teacher gives you time in class to complete an assignment take advantage of the opportunity given to you
  • Get involved
    • Joining clubs, sports, and other school sponsored activities can be a good way to make new friends
    • Some of these activities may be counted as community service hours that can be counted to your freshman community service requirement as well as to NHS
    • These activities can spice up a college application
